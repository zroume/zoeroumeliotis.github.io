About Me

I am a PhD Candidate at Trinity College Dublin in Ireland. I am working with Dr. Luca Matrà on giant impacts and extreme debris disks. Currently, I am using ALMA and VLT/CRIRES+ to characterize the millimeter dust debris and CO gas in the HD 172555 giant impact system. I am especially interested in what we can learn about the impacting planets from the debris, and how that might inform planet formation models.

I completed my Bachelor's at Smith College in Northampton, Massachusetts USA, where I double majored in astronomy and physics. There, I worked with Dr. Kimberly Ward-Duong and Dr. Kate Follette on circumsubstellar disks, and specifically how the masses of those disks are related to the mass accretion rate of the central object and how disk mass-accretion rate trends for substellar objects compare to those for stellar objects.

Outside of research, I enjoy participating in outreach opportunities, especially stargazing sessions and telescope open house nights to bring astronomy to the general public. In my free time, I enjoy reading, crocheting, and spending time in nature. I also dabble in astrophotography-- I took this photo of M13 through the eyepiece of the ARC 3.5m telescope at Apache Point Observatory.

Research

Resolving the terrestrial planet-forming region of HD 172555 with ALMA

I used data from the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) to resolve millimeter dust debris in the terrestrial region of HD 172555 for the first time. From radiative transfer modeling, I found that the peak of the millimeter dust grains is radially offset from the peak of the micron grains probed by scattered light observations. I also found that there is a break in the grain size distribution. Read more here.
